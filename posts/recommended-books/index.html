Recommended Books

It’s quite recent that some excellent bird-identification materials have moved online. Ten years ago there was approximately nothing. Now there are some very good ones. Still, much of the best and most useful information is available only in books. You won’t need these for the course, but you may want to learn more than what we cover in the course.

So, besides your Big Sibley, some of the books I value most are:

General North American guides

  • Pete Dunne’s Field Guide Companion, my favorite non-field-guide about bird identification — all text
  • Crossley ID Guide: Eastern Birds and Crossley’s other books, collaged photos, useful for learning to recognize species at various angles and distances and lighting conditions
  • National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America, easier to carry than Big Sibley, but fewer illustrations; more subspecies accounts
  • Peter Pyle Identification Guide to North American Birds, in two volumes; this is the definitive guide for identifying birds in the hand using body measurements. It is the standard reference text for bird banders (AKA “ringers”). The taxonomy is somewhat outdated.

Guides to specific North American taxa

  • The Warbler Guide, and see also its site
  • The Shorebird Guide
  • Hawks in Flight and Hawks From Every Angle
  • Peterson Guide to Seawatching
  • Petrels, Albatrosses, and Storm-Petrels of North America: A Photographic Guide

Books about observing NA birds other than identification guides

  • Peterson Reference Guide to Birding by Impression: A Different Approach to Knowing and Identifying Birds
  • The Sibley Guide to Bird Life & Behavior “provides a wealth of detail about the nesting, mating, and other behavior.” (errata)
  • Kaufman Field Guide to Advanced Birding

Textbooks

Obviously this course doesn’t use a standard ornithology textbook. If it did, I would consider ordering:

  • Ornithology: Foundation, Analysis, and Application (2018) edited by Morrison et al. John Hopkins U Press.
  • Handbook of Bird Biology, 3rd ed. (2016), ed. Lovette and Fitzpatrock. Wiley-Blackwell.

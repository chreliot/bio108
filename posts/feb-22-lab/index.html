If you haven’t read the Field Trip Instructions page yet, please do!

Plan

We’re going to focus on using our accumulating waterfowl skills on five varying freshwater bodies around Hempstead Lake State Park.

  1. We’ll probably start at Smith Pond, a ten-minute drive south of HLSP. We’ll scan the pond from the parking lot of Morgan Days Park (What3Words location: vibe.birds.rift; lat/long: 40.659928, -73.653044). ebird hotspot: https://ebird.org/hotspot/L444839.
  2. The main portion of Hempstead Lake State Park (trail map) south of the Southern State Parkway has four bodies of water ranging from small and wooded (Schodack Pond) to large and open (Hempstead Lake). We’ll look at each of them for a range of waterfowl and other freshwater-associated species like Belted Kingfisher and Great Blue Heron. We’ll see how species are associated with habitats: Wood Ducks could be on Schodack Pond, while Common Mergansers are sometimes on Hempstead Lake. Raptors and wintering passerines are likely. We use the southernmost parking lot at HLSP (What3Words location: words.open.rods; lat/long: 40.673565, -73.649833). eBird hotspot: https://ebird.org/hotspot/L476032.

Expected conditions

The weather forecast looks good: temps in 30s and 40s, mostly cloudy, no precipitation. Surfaces are ordinary: pavement, grass, and packed dirt. I haven’t checked if there is snow on the ground.

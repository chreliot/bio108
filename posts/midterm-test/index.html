Midterm Test

The midterm test on March 15 has four parts.

Parts of the test

The first part will ask you to label the names of parts of birds. The parts you may need to use are listed below. You may be given images (drawn or photographed) of a Passerine/perching bird (like on page xvi) or a duck or a hawk. Those are the groups we’ve spent time looking at. The birds in the images may be perched or in flight. To study, start with pages xvi to xxii in Sibley.

The second part will ask you to name four structural features that are distinctive of the bird shown. It will be a species that you haven’t seen yet. You should write structural features—those not having to do with color—that could help pin an identification on this unidentified bird. In other words, not something like “bill size” but rather something like “The bill is about one and a half times the length of its head.”

The third part will be a brief video of a bird in flight. It will ask you to comment on the posture and/or movement of the bird shown, such as might help identify it. As much as possible, use vocabulary we learned in class.

The fourth part will be an extended version of your weekly quizzes, except that any and all of the species we have learned so far may be on the slides. They may appear more than once, and they may not appear at all.

Relative weight

The first part will be worth 20% of the test grade. The second and third parts will each be worth 10% of the test grade. The fourth part will be worth 60% of the test grade.

Apr 26 Lab

Plan

The van is back for real (they say!), and the birds are at Hempstead Lake SP (hotspot - South End, illustrated histogram.

Target species: Swallows (Barn, Bank, Tree, and keeping an eye out for Northern Rough-winged and Cliff) and Chimney Swift from last week’s learning group, Sparrows we haven’t observed (Savannah, Swamp, Eastern Towhee), and especially a large number of arriving songbirds at the front-end of their frequency pulse (warblers, vireos, orioles, Indigo Bunting, etc.). For an idea about some of the possibilities, see the illustrated histogram and scroll up from the bottom. (And note who took the first photo at the bottom!)

See Live Migration Map and more at BirdCast

Expected conditions

As of Thursday morning, scattered thunderstorms are forecast for the beginning of lab clearing by 10 AM, though rain probability is only <50% during lab time. We can manage that. If it rains hard, we will retreat. In a little drizzle, we can probably keep going. At Hempstead, we’re never very far from the van.

Consider bringing your rain jacket, hat, or whatever keeps you comfortable in case some rain catches us.

Class Information

Our Friday classes will be at 11:15–12:40 in 103 Brower (campus map location).

Usual timeline

Our 85-minute class sessions will normally be structured like this:

  • quiz (up to 15 minutes)
  • an identification topic (about 25 minutes)
  • learning identifications (about 40 minutes)

Quiz

Each week we will have an identification quiz at the beginning of class. The quiz will consist of ten identifications. Most of the identifications will be species (or other taxa) learned the previous week. As many of two of the identifications may be from earlier weeks. I will present images, video, or (possibly, as announced) audio, and you will write the formal English name for the group on a provided sheet.

You will not be quizzed on identification below the species level (i.e., subspecies, sex age). I will be picky about spelling, punctuation, and capitalization of names. Each answer earns a full point if it gets all those things right, half a point if it points to the correct identification but gets something wrong, and no points otherwise.

Identification topics

During this first, shorter section of class, we will have an interactive presentation of one of the various dimensions of bird identification, with examples. Whenever possible, the dimension of identification will be connected with species’ behavioral ecologies.

The main topics planned are: Holistic structure-perched; Flight style; Non-flight behavior; Holistic structure-flight; Unfeathered parts; Feather colors; Interactions-conspecific; Interactions-heterospecific; Date; Geography; Immediate habitat; Doppelgängers.

Learning identifications

Use your Sibley book and other identification resources to learn to recognize images of each species to the species level. You won’t need to learn to identify birds below the species level. The list of what you’ll need to learn will be posted to the page of the week on which we covered them.
